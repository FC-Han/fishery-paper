鱼类养殖与生态保护：多学科交叉研究前沿热点

本周研究概览

本周（2026-W30）共收录渔业相关论文5篇，研究方向涵盖水产养殖营养与肠道微生物、观赏鱼体色调控、渔业补贴政策评估、大型海洋鱼类运动生态学以及鱼类早期生活史生长-摄食关系。整体研究趋势呈现以下特点：一是水产养殖领域持续关注替代蛋白源与功能性饲料的开发，并深入探究其对鱼类生理性状（如体色、肠道菌群）的影响；二是海洋生态学研究中，卫星追踪等先进技术被应用于濒危或数据缺乏物种（如 oceanic manta ray）的基础运动生态学；三是政策经济学与渔业科学的交叉研究仍受关注，但本周相关论文以评论形式出现。总体而言，研究在应用层面（养殖技术优化）和基础生态学层面均有涉及，但缺乏高影响力的综合性或突破性成果。

创新性研究

  1. Partial Replacement of Fish Meal with Fish Scale Meal Enhances Body Color Brightness and Iridophore Development in Koi (Cyprinus carpio var. koi) — 该研究的创新点在于首次系统评估了鱼鳞粉（FSM）这一水产加工副产物对观赏鱼结构色（iridophore介导的体色）的影响。传统研究多关注色素性体色，而该工作聚焦于鸟嘌呤和胶原蛋白对结构色的贡献，为“废弃物高值化利用”与“观赏鱼品质改良”提供了新思路，具有潜在的产业应用价值。
  1. First insights into oceanic manta ray movements in Japan using satellite telemetry — 该研究首次利用卫星追踪技术揭示了日本海域（该物种分布北限）oceanic manta ray的运动模式。由于该物种在西北太平洋的数据极度匮乏，此研究填补了关键知识空白，为后续保护管理（如保护区划设、渔业兼捕缓解）提供了基础生态学依据。
  1. Dynamics of Growth–Feeding Mechanisms in Japanese Anchovy Larvae Under Different Environmental Conditions — 该研究的创新点在于动态解析了环境条件如何调节鱼类仔鱼生长与摄食成功之间的耦合关系。传统“生长-存活”假说认为生长快则存活率高，但该研究揭示了这种关系的环境依赖性，挑战了单一指标预测仔鱼补充量的有效性，对渔业资源评估模型的改进具有启示意义。
  1. Community Structure and Functional Prediction of Gut Microbiota in Mandarin Fish Under Different Culture and Feeding Conditions — 该研究的创新点在于同时比较了池塘活饵、池塘人工饲料和RAS（循环水养殖系统）人工饲料三种生产模式对鳜鱼肠道微生物群落的影响。通过功能预测分析，揭示了不同养殖条件如何塑造肠道菌群的代谢潜能，为优化鳜鱼养殖模式（尤其是人工饲料驯化）提供了微生物学视角。

Partial Replacement of Fish Meal with Fish Scale Meal Enhances Body Color Brightness and Iridophore Development in Koi (Cyprinus carpio var. koi)
👤 Gao, Shengyu; Xu, Xiaolong; Ding, Tong; Cheng, Yunxin; Zhang... 📅 2026-07-20
Body color brightness is a key determinant of ornamental value in koi, yet nutritional strategies targeting structural coloration mediated by iridophores remain underexplored. Fish scale meal (FSM), an abundant fishery by-product, contains guanine and collagen, which may contribute to structural coloration and body brightness. This study evaluated the effects of partial replacement of fish meal with FSM on growth performance, body color parameters, pigment accumulation, iridophore density, and iridophore-related gene expression in koi. A total of 384 koi with an initial body weight of 19.02 ± 0.18 g were randomly assigned to six dietary treatments for 8 weeks, with FSM inclusion levels ranging from 0% to 9.55%, corresponding to 0–100% replacement of dietary fish meal. Growth performance, including final body weight, weight gain rate, and specific growth rate, decreased with increasing FSM inclusion. Body lightness (L*) and scale guanine content increased progressively with FSM level and reached their highest values at 9.55% FSM, whereas redness (a*), yellowness (b*), and total carotenoid content showed the opposite trend. Iridophore density was significantly higher in the 3.82–5.73% FSM groups than in the other treatments. The expression of iridophore-related genes, including sox10, pax3, ltk, alx4a, and foxd3, showed non-linear responses and generally reached higher levels at moderate FSM inclusion, whereas pka showed an opposite transcript pattern. Overall, dietary FSM inclusion elicited trait-dependent responses in koi. Low or no FSM inclusion was preferable for maintaining growth, intermediate inclusion was more favorable for iridophore development, whereas higher FSM inclusion enhanced brightness-related traits. These findings suggest that FSM should be used according to the intended production objective, with inclusion levels adjusted for growth maintenance, iridophore-related coloration improvement, or brightness enhancement.

Sexual Segregation of Blacktip Reef Sharks (Carcharhinus melanopterus) at Mitita Island, South Morotai, North Maluku, Indonesia
👤 Danendra, Muhammad Raissa; Sunarto; Ismail, Mochamad Rudyans... 📅 2026-07-20
Sexual segregation may influence habitat use, social interactions, and population organisation in reef-associated sharks. However, habitat-based and social segregation in blacktip reef sharks (Carcharhinus melanopterus) at Mitita Island remains insufficiently understood. This study investigated habitat-based and social patterns of sexual segregation in blacktip reef sharks (Carcharhinus melanopterus) at Mitita Island, South Morotai, North Maluku. Field observations were conducted at Mitita Point and Niomi Point over eight sampling days from June to August 2024, comprising 24 dives. Individual sharks were recognised through photo-identification using natural markings on the dorsal and caudal fins. Environmental conditions, benthic cover, reef fish abundance, shark occurrence, and agonistic interactions were recorded. Eighteen individuals were identified, including 12 females and 6 males. Females occurred more frequently at Mitita Point, which had greater live coral cover, higher reef fish abundance, and calmer current conditions, whereas males were observed more frequently at Niomi Point. Exploratory Poisson regression indicated that current velocity score and salinity were associated with female occurrence; none of the measured environmental variables significantly predicted male occurrence. Analysis of “Give Way” interactions suggested that females occupied more central positions within the observed interaction network. The findings indicate sex-related differences in spatial occurrence and social interactions within the local aggregation. However, these patterns should be interpreted cautiously because the study involved a small identified population, two sampling stations, a short observation period, an observational design, and controlled feeding during diver-based surveys. Longer-term, seasonal, non-provisioned monitoring and movement-tracking approaches are required to determine whether the observed patterns persist across time and habitats.

👤 Pereira, amíria Bárbara Brito; Silva, Eduarda Fernanda de Ol... 📅 2026-07-21
This study characterizes the bycatch associated with Arapaima gigas fisheries in the Cautário River, Rondônia, Brazil, where the species occurs as an invasive species. Fisheries monitoring was conducted during the low-water period (August to October 2025), when A. gigas capture rates intensify. During this period, 69 bycatch individuals were recorded, belonging to nine species, distributed in seven families and eight orders. Vertebrates were predominant, with reptiles and fish accounting for more than 68% of the incidental captures. The turtle Podocnemis unifilis was the most frequent species (36.23%), followed by the fish Colossoma macropomum (31.88%) and Phractocephalus hemioliopterus (11.59%). The results indicated that the high taxonomic diversity and representation of non-target species reflect the low selectivity of the fishing gear used in A. gigas fisheries. These findings highlight the importance of continuous monitoring of the bycatch as an essential tool for the adaptive management of A. gigas fisheries in the Cautário River, part of the Guaporé River basin.

Stage of bloom succession drives trophic marker variability in sea ice algal communities in Hudson Bay
👤 Leu, Eva; Campbell, Karley; Fahl, Kirsten; Brown, Thomas; We... 📅 2026-07-20
Sea ice algae are a vital food source in Arctic marine ecosystems, yet the variability of lipid- and isotope-based trophic markers within natural sea ice algal communities remains poorly understood and challenges the use of trophic marker approaches. This study tracked a bottom sea ice algal bloom in northwestern Hudson Bay, examining how environmental factors influence polyunsaturated fatty acids (PUFAs), highly branched isoprenoids (HBIs), and δ¹³C ratios of fatty acids. Trophic markers showed high spatiotemporal variability that was significantly correlated with sea ice nutrients and dissolved inorganic carbon (DIC) concentrations. Early bloom stages were characterized by high nutrient levels, peak bloom samples by highest percentages of PUFAs, while late stages showed increased 16:1(n-7) levels, suggesting storage lipid formation. Gradually increasing DIC depletion corresponded with enriched δ¹³C signals. The highest IP25 values occurred at nearshore sites where bloom progression was least advanced, suggesting possible benthic-sympagic coupling. Bloom succession stage differed between sampling sites and was the dominant factor influencing trophic marker variability leading to spatial variability along the transect.

Invasive hybridization has unpredicted and variable effects on trout survival in contrasting environments
👤 Strait, Jeffrey T.; Kovach, Ryan; Eby, Lisa A.; Muhlfeld, Cl... 📅 2026-07-20
Invasive hybridization between native and introduced species is widespread, especially in fishes, yet its effects on fitness-related traits across environments remain poorly understood. We combined individual genetic admixture estimates with capture–recapture data to quantify how hybridization between native westslope cutthroat trout (Oncorhynchus lewisi) and invasive rainbow trout (Oncorhynchus mykiss) influences survival. We measured individual survival of 5,186 trout across three populations in contrasting environments. Non-native admixture significantly affected survival in two populations, with positive effects during summer and negative effects during winter. Prior research has shown hybridization increases with stream temperature, however, survival patterns did not follow this trend. In a warmer stream, native trout in all size-classes had ≥20% higher annual survival than hybrids, whereas in a cooler stream juvenile native trout had ≥20% lower survival than hybrids. These findings indicate that hybridization effects on survival are complex and vary among populations, environments, and size-classes. Landscape patterns of hybridization may therefore not reflect local survival (or fitness) outcomes, emphasizing that measures fitness-related traits across populations and environments could elucidate the eco-evolutionary consequences of invasive hybridization.

Comment on “On the pass‐through rate of the Nutrition North Canada subsidy”
👤 Galloway, Tracey; Li, Nicholas 📅 2026-07-20
Abstract Naylor, Deaton and Ker provide estimates of Nutrition North Canada subsidy pass‐through using data we collected and an identification strategy we devised. They propose an alternative econometric specification based on estimating passthrough for separate monopoly and non‐monopoly sub‐samples and aggregating the coefficients using subsidy share weights. They argue that this specification is more appropriate for estimating overall subsidy pass‐through and conclude that its application to our data provides evidence for full subsidy pass‐through. We show that their conclusions are not warranted by making two distinct but related points. First, we show that conclusions based on their specification are not robust to two changes in the estimating sample. Correcting two subsidy assignment and one monopoly classification error in the original data set, or using a time period more relevant for comparing heterogeneous effects across monopoly and non‐monopoly communities, leads to estimates that either reinforce our original finding of incomplete passthrough or that are too imprecise to be informative. Second, we provide a graphical illustration and power calculation to make a more general point about the appropriateness and limitations of the type of sub‐sample analysis they propose in settings with limited sample size and statistical power.

Biological, freshwater, and marine drivers of age at maturity in wild Chinook Salmon
👤 Gosselin, Jennifer; Sandford, Benjamin P; O'Brien, Caitlin S... 📅 2026-07-20
Understanding variation in age at maturity is important for endangered species recovery because older, larger adults contribute disproportionately to the next generation. Conditions in early life stages may have underappreciated impacts on age at maturity. Our study objective was to associate adult age of individually tagged wild, spring/summer Chinook salmon (Oncorhynchus tshawytscha) outmigrating from the Snake River (Idaho and Washington, USA) in 1998–2020 with covariates measured during juvenile and subadult stages. We used a hierarchical Bayesian ordinal probit regression model to estimate statistical effects of juvenile body length, seasonal migration timing or river temperature, transported or in-river hydrosystem passage, river flow, and a large-scale ocean index. Results indicated notable carryover effects consistent with underlying biological mechanisms related to growth and development, in which shorter juvenile length and later seasonal migration timing were associated with older adults. These biological and behavioural factors were more important than riverine or marine environmental conditions examined. Our study suggests that managers and decision makers should consider carryover effects from the juvenile life stage on age structure in conjunction with survival.

Effects of experimentally manipulated cortisol and nutrition on wound healing capacity in a teleost fish
👤 Zolderdo, Aaron; Reid, C.H.; Lawrence, Michael; Algera, Dirk... 📅 2026-07-20
Wound healing is a complex multi-system process that enables organs and tissues to re-establish their form and function post-injury. Exposure to stress and nutritional restrictions results in energetic trade-offs at a physiological level, usually at the expense of immunity and tissue repair. Relatively few studies have focused on how stress and nutrition mediate immune function and healing capacity in fishes. We subjected wild-caught pumpkinseed (Lepomis gibbosus (Linnaeus, 1758)) to a subcutaneous incision (a standardized wound) while experimentally manipulating plasma cortisol levels (via a slow-release cortisol implant) and nutrition (via restricting food intake) to evaluate how these affected wound healing over time (2, 7, and 14 d). Wound healing was poorest for cortisol-treated fed fish at 7 d post-implant. While cortisol implants resulted in lymphocyte apoptosis and neutropenia, which are well-described immunological responses to increased cortisol concentrations, fasting had little effect. Reproductively active males had different leukocyte counts than non-reproductive males, though the direction varied with leukocyte type, suggesting a trade-off in immune response during reproduction. The negative influence of cortisol on tissue repair and inflammation aligns with the wound healing literature across taxa. However, the ability of fasting to reduce these negative effects was unexpected and deserves further study.

👤 Yan, Lulu; Su, Jiaqi; Zhu, Changbo 📅 2026-07-20
Nanoplastic particles are increasingly detected in aquatic environments, yet whether cellular stress responses persist after exposure cessation remains unclear. Here, Lateolabrax maculatus rostral (LMR) cells from spotted sea bass were continuously exposed for up to 30 passages (105 days) to nominal 20 nm carboxylated fluorescent polystyrene particles (20 μg/mL), followed by 10 particle-free passages (recovery). Long-term exposure was associated with reduced proliferation and pronounced changes in cell surface morphology and ultrastructure. Although NP-associated fluorescence became undetectable during recovery, several nuclear-associated alterations persisted throughout the 10-passage recovery period, including TEM-observed nuclear-envelope alterations, filamentous actin (F-actin) reorganization, and sustained elevation of phosphorylated H2AX (γ-H2AX) foci. Several nuclear pore complex (NPC) genes were downregulated during exposure and rebounded after particle removal, whereas the nuclear-to-cytoplasmic distribution of proliferating cell nuclear antigen (PCNA) remained shifted. Together, these findings indicate that prolonged exposure to this nanoscale polystyrene particle formulation was associated with nuclear stress responses that did not fully resolve within the 10-passage recovery window in fish cells. Because bulk-polymer and chemical-extract (leachate) controls were not included, nanoscale-specific effects cannot be distinguished from contributions of polymer chemistry, surface functionalization, fluorescent dye, or other formulation-related effects.

The Paradox of Availability: A Quantitative Analysis of Veterinary Medicinal Products for Aquaculture in Europe Between 2018 and 2026
👤 Chambers, Lauren; Jansen, Wiebke; Palić, Dušan; Fabris, Andr... 📅 2026-07-20
Availability of veterinary medicinal products is a critical determinant of health, welfare, and economic sustainability in European aquaculture. This study assessed the changes in the authorization and reported market availability of veterinary medicines for finfish in the European Union (EU) and European Free Trade Association (EFTA) between 2018 and 2026, by comparing and analyzing data on veterinary medicinal products for aquatic species from the Heads of Medicines Agencies (HMA), the FishMedPlus Coalition, and the European Medicines Agency Union Product Database. The results revealed a wide divergence between regulatory authorization and reported availability. While the total number of authorized VMPs nominally increased from 304 in 2018 to 332 in 2025 (+9.2%), the number of reported unique active substances available declined (−16.7%). Crucially, the analysis of 2025 data indicates that only 26.5% of authorized products were reported to be actively available on the market, creating a “paper market” that masks severe therapeutic gaps, particularly in minor fish species such as seabass, seabream, carp, and trout sectors. Potential key drivers identified were limited markets, industry consolidation, increased burden related to stricter environmental requirements, as well as enlarged administrative and financial burdens. Despite EU legislative mechanisms designed to support limited markets, the sector faces stagnation in therapeutic diversity, necessitating continued reliance on the off-label use of medicines.
