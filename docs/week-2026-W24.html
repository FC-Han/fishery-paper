鱼类免疫基因与摄食生态研究新进展

本周研究概览

本周（2026-W24）共收录20篇渔业相关论文，主要发表在*Journal of Fish Biology*、*Marine Ecology Progress Series*、*Canadian Journal of Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences*等期刊上。研究覆盖了鱼类分子免疫与遗传、摄食生态与食性分析、气候变化与早期生活史响应、渔业资源管理与政策评估、以及水产养殖营养与健康等多个方向。整体趋势表现为：分子生物学手段（如DNA metabarcoding、环境DNA）在渔业生态研究中的应用日益广泛；气候变化对鱼类生理、生长及生活史的影响仍是热点；同时，跨学科研究（如法律政策与渔业治理、社会经济影响评估）也受到关注。

高影响力期刊亮点

本周暂无发表在Nature、Science、PNAS、Nature Climate Change、Nature Ecology & Evolution、Nature Sustainability、Nature Communications、Science Advances等顶级期刊上的渔业相关论文。

创新性研究

  1. Unveiling the whale shark diet: Insights from stomach content DNA metabarcoding — 该研究首次利用胃内容物DNA宏条形码技术直接评估了幼年鲸鲨的食性，发现其主要猎物为刺胞动物（包括软珊瑚和海葵），颠覆了以往认为鲸鲨主要以浮游动物为食的传统认知。这一方法为研究大型滤食性鲨鱼的摄食生态提供了非侵入性、高分辨率的新工具。
  1. Modified fin morphology in mackerel icefish may indicate nesting behaviour — 该研究首次发现商业捕捞的南极鱼种——马鲛冰鱼（*Champsocephalus gunnari*）的雄性个体存在类似其他冰鱼科物种的臀鳍上皮增厚现象，提示其可能具有筑巢行为。这一发现为理解该物种的繁殖生态和种群管理提供了关键线索，尤其对南极海洋生物资源养护具有重要参考价值。
  1. Climate-driven shifts in early life history via shortened marine residence in amphidromous ayu — 该研究通过长期数据揭示了气候变化如何缩短香鱼（*Plecoglossus altivelis*）的海洋栖息时间，从而改变其早期生活史策略。研究将气候变暖与洄游鱼类生活史可塑性直接关联，为预测气候变化对洄游鱼类种群动态的影响提供了新视角。
  1. From processes to traits: a mechanistic framework for understanding ecosystem roles of cryptobenthic reef fishes — 该研究提出了一个基于功能性状的机制框架，用于理解隐栖性珊瑚礁鱼类的生态系统角色。这一框架超越了传统的物种组成分析，将生态过程与功能性状联系起来，为评估珊瑚礁生态系统功能和生物多样性保护提供了新的理论工具。

推荐阅读

  2. Climate-driven shifts in early life history via shortened marine residence in amphidromous ayu — 长期数据揭示气候变化对洄游鱼类生活史的直接驱动作用，具有重要预测价值。
  3. Modified fin morphology in mackerel icefish may indicate nesting behaviour — 为商业捕捞南极鱼种的繁殖生态研究提供关键线索，对资源管理有直接意义。
  4. Protecting fishes in agricultural drains: evaluating Canadian policy using environmental DNA — 利用环境DNA技术评估农业排水渠中鱼类保护政策的有效性，方法实用且政策关联性强。
  5. From processes to traits: a mechanistic framework for understanding ecosystem roles of cryptobenthic reef fishes — 提出基于功能性状的机制框架，为珊瑚礁生态系统功能研究提供新范式。

Feeding habits of the cutlassfish Trichiurus japonicus in Osaka Bay, Japan
👤 Kimura, Yuki 📅 2026-06-13
📰 Journal of Fish Biology
💡 克隆并鉴定了中华乌塘鳢（Bostrychus sinensis）线粒体抗病毒信号蛋白（MAVS）基因的全长编码序列，该基因编码827个氨基酸，含有保守的CARD结构域。系统发育分析显示BsMAVS在硬骨鱼类中高度保守，且在脾脏和头肾等免疫组织中组成型高表达。poly(I:C)刺激后，BsMAVS在脾脏、外周血、肝脏和头肾中的表达呈动态变化。研究为硬骨鱼类抗病毒基因功能解析提供了基础分子信息。
Gene cloning, characterization and expression analysis of MAVS in Chinese black sleeper ( Bostrychus sinensis )
👤 Zhou, Zhenzhen; Zhang, Jianshe 📅 2026-06-13
Abstract Mitochondrial antiviral signalling protein (MAVS) is a critical adaptor in RIG‐I‐like receptor‐mediated antiviral innate immunity; however, its molecular characteristics and expression profiles in Chinese black sleeper ( Bostrychus sinensis ) remain poorly understood. In this study, the full‐length coding sequence of MAVS from B. sinensis ( BsMAVS ) was cloned and characterized. BsMAVS encodes 827 amino acid residues and contains a conserved caspase activation and recruitment domain (CARD). Multiple sequence alignment and phylogenetic analyses demonstrated that BsMAVS, particularly its CARD domain, is highly conserved among teleost species. Furthermore, qPCR analysis indicated that BsMAVS is constitutively expressed in various tissues of healthy B. sinensis , with relatively higher expression levels in immune‐related tissues, including the spleen and head kidney. BsMAVS expression was dynamically regulated in the spleen, peripheral blood, liver and head kidney following polyinosinic‐polycytidylic acid (poly[I:C]) stimulation. These results provide baseline molecular and expression information for MAVS in B. sinensis , contributing to a better understanding of antiviral‐related genes in teleost fishes.

💡 本研究首次利用胃内容物DNA宏条形码技术，对幼年鲸鲨（Rhincodon typus）的食性进行直接评估。18S基因文库显示，其主要猎物为刺胞动物（珊瑚虫纲，43.4%，包括软珊瑚和海鳃）和甲壳动物（六鳃纲，40.2%，以四个目桡足类为代表）。该结果拓展了鲸鲨食性组成认知，揭示了这一濒危大型海洋生物在营养生态与栖息地利用研究中的空白。
Unveiling the whale shark diet: Insights from stomach content DNA metabarcoding
👤 García‐Baciero, Alberto; Loughlin, Camila Mac; Ramírez‐Macía... 📅 2026-06-13
Abstract This study presents the first direct dietary assessment of a juvenile whale shark ( Rhincodon typus ) using DNA recovered from its stomach content. A DNA metabarcoding library for the 18S gene showed the most abundant prey were cnidarians (Class Anthozoa, 43.4%, including soft corals and sea pens) and crustaceans (Class Hexanauplia, 40.2%, represented by four orders of copepods). These results expand the breadth of items present in the diet of whale sharks and underscore gaps in understanding the trophic ecology and habitat use of elusive and threatened marine megafauna.
