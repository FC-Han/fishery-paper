渔业科学前沿：基因、生态与可持续管理热点透视

本周研究概览

本周（2026-W24）共收录20篇渔业相关论文，主要发表在Journal of Fish Biology、Marine Ecology Progress Series、Fisheries Science、Canadian Journal of Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences等期刊上。研究方向涵盖鱼类分子免疫与生理（如MAVS基因克隆、饲料添加剂效果）、渔业生态与行为（如鲸鲨食性、带鱼摄食、冰鱼繁殖行为）、气候变化影响（如温度对鱼类生长脂质的影响、气候驱动的早期生活史变化）、渔业管理与治理（如朝鲜渔业法、社会经济评估、环境DNA监测）以及海洋生态过程（如浮游生物垂直分布、溶解有机碳释放）。整体趋势显示，分子生物学与生态学交叉研究持续活跃，气候变化对渔业物种的生理和生态影响成为热点，同时渔业治理与保护管理研究也受到关注。

创新性研究

  1. Unveiling the whale shark diet: Insights from stomach content DNA metabarcoding — 该研究首次利用胃内容物DNA宏条形码技术直接评估鲸鲨（*Rhincodon typus*）的食性，发现其主要猎物为刺胞动物（包括软珊瑚和海葵），颠覆了传统认为鲸鲨以浮游动物为主的认知。这一方法为研究大型滤食性动物的食性提供了非侵入性、高分辨率的新工具。
  1. Modified fin morphology in mackerel icefish (*Champsocephalus gunnari*) may indicate nesting behaviour — 该研究首次发现商业捕捞的南极鱼类——马鲛冰鱼的雄性个体存在类似其他冰鱼科物种的臀鳍上皮增厚现象，提示其可能具有筑巢行为。这一发现为理解南极鱼类的繁殖生态提供了新线索，对渔业管理具有潜在意义。
  1. Climate‐driven shifts in early life history via shortened marine residence in amphidromous ayu *Plecoglossus altivelis* — 该研究通过长期数据揭示了气候变暖如何缩短香鱼（*Plecoglossus altivelis*）的海洋滞留时间，从而改变其早期生活史策略。这一发现强调了气候变化对洄游鱼类生活史关键阶段的深刻影响，为预测未来种群动态提供了重要依据。
  1. Integrating habitat connectivity and fishing pressure to promote migratory marine species conservation — 该研究提出了一个整合栖息地连通性与捕捞压力的框架，用于促进洄游海洋物种的保护。其创新之处在于将空间生态学与渔业管理相结合，为制定更有效的保护策略提供了可操作的分析工具。
  1. From processes to traits: a mechanistic framework for understanding ecosystem roles of cryptobenthic reef fishes — 该研究构建了一个从生态过程到功能性状的机制框架，用于理解隐栖礁鱼类的生态系统角色。这一框架弥补了传统研究对小型、隐蔽性鱼类功能认知的不足，为珊瑚礁生态系统功能研究提供了新视角。

Availability of dark daytime refuge may limit mysid abundance in the Laurentian Great Lakes
👤 Nasworthy, Kayden; Watkins, James M; Evans, Thomas M.; Blair... 📅 2026-06-12
The zooplankton Mysis diluviana is a major component of the Laurentian Great Lakes food web and has recently declined in abundance in both lakes Michigan and Huron. The drivers of these declines are not well understood. Here, we explore the hypothesis that recent increases in water clarity have contributed to the decline of M. diluviana (mysids) by limiting the availability of daytime dark refuge from visual predators. Using Secchi depth data from 1996-2021, we estimate that dark refuge has decreased substantially in lakes Michigan and Huron where mysids have declined, but dark refuge has remained more stable in Ontario and Superior where mysid populations did not decline. Results from a 2021 uncrewed surface vessel hydroacoustic survey and lake-wide net-based sampling in lakes Michigan and Huron revealed significantly more mysids in areas with dark refuge, such as Lake Michigan's northern basin. Conversely, Lake Huron contains sparse dark refuge consistent with low mysid densities in that lake. Higher water clarity leading to increased predation may be a primary driver of mysid declines in the Great Lakes.

⭐ 📰 Canadian J. Fisheries & Aquatic Sciences
Protecting fishes in agricultural drains: evaluating Canadian policy using environmental DNA
👤 Morphet, Markelle E.; Van Nynatten, Alexander; Yates, Matthe... 📅 2026-06-11
Diverse aquatic communities inhabit agricultural drains, but maintenance of these drains can harm fishes and their habitats. In Canada, drains are classified based on their fish assemblages to protect sensitive species by managing drain-maintenance activities. Current Fisheries and Oceans Canada (DFO) fish sampling is challenged by environmental constraints and limited resources. To evaluate the effectiveness of current DFO methods to classify drains, we compared species detected by DFO methods (dip nets, electrofishing, seines) and by environmental DNA (eDNA) metabarcoding with two primers. Environmental DNA detected more species (n=28) than the DFO methods (n=18) at 59% of drain segments, resulting in changes to 10 of 29 drain classifications. These results suggest that current DFO methods are insufficient to classify drains and, hence, adequately protect sensitive fishes and fish habitat within them. While we show the potential of eDNA to detect fish species in drains, both methods may be susceptible to imperfect detection resulting in incorrect drain classification. Therefore, studies should conduct occupancy modelling and cost analyses for both methods to determine the optimal sampling strategy for drain classification.

⭐ 📰 Frontiers in Marine Science
North Korea’s fisheries law and cross-border fisheries governance: a doctrinal assessment of domestic control and regional transparency
👤 Hong, Dae Un 📅 2026-06-12
Large-scale illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing in waters claimed by the Democratic People’s Republic of Korea (DPRK) has been documented mainly through satellite analysis and sanctions-enforcement studies. However, these approaches reveal little about how the DPRK’s own law structures authority, access, inspection, and legal responsibility. This article uses doctrinal legal analysis to examine the DPRK Fisheries Law using the accessible 2015 and 2022 consolidated texts, the 20 May 2025 amendment—used as a confirmatory and contextual extension—and the broader amendment history reflected in earlier legal materials. The statute is assessed across four governance pillars—conservation and sustainable use; monitoring, control, and surveillance; access authorization and foreign-vessel control; and sanctions and deterrence—against differentiated benchmarks drawn from the United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea, the Port State Measures Agreement, the FAO Code of Conduct for Responsible Fisheries, and fisheries-related UN sanctions. The 2015 and 2022 texts show partial alignment in conservation planning, protected-resource management, and formal authorization, but persistent gaps remain in monitoring infrastructure, inspection traceability, foreign-access transparency, and cooperative information sharing. While the 2025 amendment confirms a further move toward denser domestic legal responsibility, including direct stoppage measures, steeper fines, and a more differentiated sanction ladder, it lacks a parallel advance in monitoring or transparency. The DPRK framework therefore operates more as an instrument of domestic administrative control than as an integrated component of regional fisheries governance. By grounding this conclusion in the statute’s own internal evolution rather than in vessel-behavior evidence alone, the article provides a more comprehensive legal baseline for future research on fisheries governance in sanctioned, low-transparency settings.
📰 Journal of Fish Biology
Gene cloning, characterization and expression analysis of MAVS in Chinese black sleeper ( Bostrychus sinensis )
👤 Zhou, Zhenzhen; Zhang, Jianshe 📅 2026-06-13
Abstract Mitochondrial antiviral signalling protein (MAVS) is a critical adaptor in RIG‐I‐like receptor‐mediated antiviral innate immunity; however, its molecular characteristics and expression profiles in Chinese black sleeper ( Bostrychus sinensis ) remain poorly understood. In this study, the full‐length coding sequence of MAVS from B. sinensis ( BsMAVS ) was cloned and characterized. BsMAVS encodes 827 amino acid residues and contains a conserved caspase activation and recruitment domain (CARD). Multiple sequence alignment and phylogenetic analyses demonstrated that BsMAVS, particularly its CARD domain, is highly conserved among teleost species. Furthermore, qPCR analysis indicated that BsMAVS is constitutively expressed in various tissues of healthy B. sinensis , with relatively higher expression levels in immune‐related tissues, including the spleen and head kidney. BsMAVS expression was dynamically regulated in the spleen, peripheral blood, liver and head kidney following polyinosinic‐polycytidylic acid (poly[I:C]) stimulation. These results provide baseline molecular and expression information for MAVS in B. sinensis , contributing to a better understanding of antiviral‐related genes in teleost fishes.

📰 Journal of Fish Biology
Temperature, but not acidification, influences the growth and lipid profile of juvenile sand whiting, Sillago ciliata (Cuvier 1829)
👤 Shalders, Tanika C.; Tan, Melissa; Hall, Karina C.; Coleman,... 📅 2026-06-12
Abstract Seafood provides an essential source of macro‐ and micronutrients for coastal communities worldwide. Climate change is a key threat to seafood security, altering the sizes, abundances, distributions, physiology and ecological interactions of fisheries species, and increasingly, there is evidence of impacts to seafood nutritional quality. In a 12‐week mesocosm experiment, we tested the influence of projected ocean warming and acidification scenarios on the growth and lipid quality of juvenile sand whiting ( Sillago ciliata ), a popular fisheries species in eastern Australia. The growth of S. ciliata significantly increased (by 61% body weight) under elevated temperature (+3°C) but was not affected by acidification treatment levels. Lipidomic analysis revealed no influence of temperature or acidification on total lipid content or the composition and total proportions of lipid classes and subclasses. However, elevated temperatures significantly impacted the overall composition of fatty acids, including a shift toward higher saturation and a decline in important omega‐3 fatty acids. Fish exposed to elevated temperature treatments had more saturated fatty acids than those at control temperatures, along with reduced levels of the valuable omega‐3 eicosapentaenoic (C20:5) and docosahexaenoic (C22:6) fatty acids. Despite impacting fatty acid composition in S. ciliata, the increased growth of the juvenile whiting, if sustained into adulthood, under elevated temperatures, may help compensate for the overall availability of essential polyunsaturated fatty acids to support consumer nutritional requirements. These findings contribute to the growing body of evidence on variable climate resilience in nearshore species to future environmental conditions and the implications for the trophic transfer of nutrients in estuarine ecosystems.

Dietary sodium propionate enhances growth performance, digestive enzymes, haematology and immunity in Wallago attu
👤 Tanveer, Ayesha; Khan, Noor; Fatima, Mahroze; Shakir, Hafiz ... 📅 2026-06-12
Abstract Sodium propionate (SP), a short‐chain fatty acid, has been widely used as a feed additive to improve fish growth and health status. The current experiment was designed to investigate the effects of dietary SP on growth, enzymatic activities, haematology and immune status of Wallago attu . Fingerlings (initial weight = 8.80 ± 0.58 g) were fed with graded levels of SP comprising 0.0, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 4.0 and 8.0 g/kg levels for 60 days in triplicate glass aquaria (89 × 58 × 61 cm [length × width × depth]). The results showed significant ( p < 0.05) improvement in growth parameters such as weight gain (%), specific growth rate and feed conversion ratio up to 4 g/kg SP. Moreover, the whole‐body proximate composition (crude protein and lipid content) and antioxidant enzymes (superoxide dismutase, catalase, glutathione peroxidase) showed significant improvements up to 4 g/kg. However, the digestive enzyme activities (amylase, protease, lipase) increased significantly at 2 g/kg, while decreased malondialdehyde content was observed at 2–4 g/kg. Furthermore, the haematological (haemoglobin, haematocrit, red blood cells and white blood cells counts) and nonspecific immune parameters (serum total protein, lysozyme and IgM) improved significantly up to 4 g/kg and decreased thereafter. Moreover, Polynomial regression analysis showed that 5.88 g/kg SP is the optimum requirement for the growth of W. attu fingerlings when fed with graded levels of SP. In conclusion, the SP supplementation in W. attu showed best results ranged between 2 and 4 g/kg level by improving fish health and growth performance.

📰 Journal of Fish Biology
Climate‐driven shifts in early life history via shortened marine residence in amphidromous ayu Plecoglossus altivelis
👤 Nagayama, Shigeya; Fujii, Ryouji; Harada, Morihiro; Ohara, K... 📅 2026-06-11
Abstract Climate warming is altering thermal regimes across freshwater and marine ecosystems, with important consequences for fish life‐history schedules, particularly during early life stages that span connected habitats. We characterized long‐term variation in multiple early life‐history traits and examined how thermal factors relate to shifts in the early life‐history schedule of amphidromous ayu Plecoglossus altivelis . Individuals initiating spring upstream migration earlier were hatched earlier and were larger in body size across brood years, consistent with established patterns. In contrast, age at migration showed no consistent relationship with migration timing, indicating that variation in marine residence duration is not solely determined by migration phenology but is influenced by conditions experienced during the marine phase. Over the past three decades, age at spring migration declined significantly, indicating progressive shortening of the marine residence period. This decline was closely associated with elevated winter seawater temperature, whereas riverine thermal indicators representing the onset and termination of marine residence showed no comparable relationship. Although delayed autumn reproduction has been reported under warming river conditions, enhanced winter growth in the marine environment may offset these effects, resulting in little long‐term change in migration timing. Overall, this study provides evidence that climate‐driven changes in coastal marine thermal conditions are associated with shifts in marine residence duration and early life‐history schedules in amphidromous fishes, highlighting the importance of integrated freshwater–marine thermal regimes under ongoing climate warming.
